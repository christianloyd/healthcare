Introduction
Healthcare encompasses the organized provision of medical services, preventive care, and health promotion activities that work together to maintain and improve population health outcomes. Health centers serve as the primary point of contact between patients and healthcare services, making efficient record management essential for maintaining continuity of care and supporting public health initiatives (Srivastava et al., 2020). Healthcare record management is the foundation of quality medical service delivery, particularly in maternal and child health programs, where accurate tracking of prenatal visits and immunization schedules has a direct impact on patient outcomes. Effective recordkeeping systems enable healthcare providers to monitor patient progress, ensure continuity of care, maintain compliance with health protocols, and support evidence-based decision-making in clinical settings. The transition from traditional paper-based documentation to digital health information systems has become essential for healthcare facilities seeking to improve service quality and operational efficiency. Modern healthcare delivery increasingly depends on reliable information systems that provide real-time access to patient data while maintaining data security and supporting clinical workflows (Masuku, 2019). Digital health record systems, particularly in maternal and child health services, benefit from enabling the systematic tracking of prenatal care visits, vaccination schedules, and health outcomes across extended care periods.
The Barangay Health Center of Mecolong in Zamboanga del Sur is a primary healthcare facility serving the barangay's community with essential health services, including maternal care and pediatric immunization programs. Established to provide accessible healthcare to the rural population, the facility has maintained its commitment in delivering preventive and curative services through various health programs mandated by the Department of Health. The health center is vital to the local healthcare infrastructure, providing essential medical services to residents. It focuses particularly on maternal and child health initiatives that underpin the delivery of preventive healthcare in the rural community. 
Currently, the health center relies on manual recording, with Barangay Health Workers maintaining handwritten logbooks to track patient information, appointment schedules, and immunization records. Healthcare staff manually enter patient names, ages, scheduled visits, and treatment remarks into physical logbooks. At the same time, prenatal and immunization tracking depend on entries made in Mother's Record Books and Immunization Cards that patients must bring to each visit. The monitoring process involves registering patients in logbooks, scheduling checkups and vaccinations, and conducting home visits when patients fail to appear at the center. Staff members rely on manual data entry in patient cards to track upcoming appointments, though this system often falls short of ensuring consistent patient follow-up.
According to Jessa Abella Macarate (personal communication, July 23, 2025), a Barangay Health Worker at the health center, the manual documentation system poses significant operational challenges that affect healthcare delivery and patient outcomes. She explained that one of their biggest struggles are keeping records by hand, noting that it is time-consuming and prone to errors. Macarate shared how difficult it is to find past patient records, describing how they often have to go through piles of documents or search through different files, which slows down their other essential tasks. The healthcare worker also mentioned that creating monthly reports is time-consuming, as they must collect data from multiple sources and carefully review everything. If they make even one mistake, they must start over, wasting valuable time that could be spent on patient care. Furthermore, the manual system lacks the capability to send automated appointment reminders to patients. As a result, patients often forget their appointment dates, despite manually entering them in their personal health records, which can lead to gaps in prenatal care and delayed immunizations. These operational inefficiencies create barriers to the effective delivery of maternal and child health services in the community.
A web-based system of prenatal and immunization record addresses these challenges by providing digital access to patient profiles, appointments, and service histories. BHWs and health staff can register patients, update medical information, and monitor schedules from any device with internet access. Automatic notifications remind mothers of upcoming checkups or vaccinations via SMS, eliminating the appointment-tracking problems currently faced in the manual system (Louw et al., 2024). The digital platform eliminates the need to search through physical logbooks, ensuring patient records remain accessible even when staff are unavailable or documents are misplaced. Digital health record systems have demonstrated effectiveness in community health settings by reducing appointment dropouts, improving patient follow-up rates, and enabling healthcare workers to manage larger patient loads more efficiently (Sarkar et al., 2020). Healthcare workers can automatically generate monthly reports from the system database, eliminating the time-consuming process of manually collecting and verifying data across multiple sources.
This digital transformation represents a strategic advancement in rural healthcare delivery that aligns with modern health information system standards while addressing the specific operational needs of the Barangay Mecolong health center. By replacing manual processes with an integrated web-based platform, the system will enhance the quality of maternal and child health services while reducing the administrative burden on healthcare staff. The implementation will ensure data security and operational continuity, supporting the health center's mission to provide reliable, accessible healthcare services to the community.
Project Context
This project addresses inefficiencies in manual prenatal and immunization record management at the Barangay Mecolong Health Center, including physical record storage and tracking issues experienced by healthcare workers and patients. The Barangay Health Workers and midwives using this system will be able to manage patient records digitally and provide real-time updates on appointments and immunization schedules, eliminating the need for handwritten logbooks. Additionally, the healthcare facility can transition from traditional paper-based recordkeeping to a digital approach, reducing administrative workload and eliminating the risk of lost or damaged patient records. Prenatal and immunization record management in a healthcare facility aims to create a system that maximizes efficiency and meets quality standards while operating under operational constraints. Implementing a web-based system of prenatal and immunization record will enable prenatal monitoring and immunization tracking with digital storage of health records, addressing concerns about slow processing and poor communication among healthcare workers and patients. 
The proposed system will provide a digital platform and communication tool to assist midwives and barangay health workers in managing their patient care responsibilities with greater ease and simplicity. Healthcare workers can track patient progress in real time, automatically send appointment reminders, and access complete patient histories without searching through physical files. Meanwhile, patients will receive timely notifications about their scheduled checkups and vaccinations, reducing missed appointments and ensuring continuity of care.
Purpose and Description
This research aims to develop a web-based system of prenatal and immunization record, providing healthcare workers with efficient patient record management and a digital platform for prenatal monitoring and immunization tracking. With this system, healthcare facilities can streamline patient monitoring, store patient health records, and reduce administrative burdens. 
The system requires user authentication and role-based access to ensure data security and appropriate system usage.
1. Midwife. Has the ability to oversee prenatal monitoring and manage patient appointments effectively. The digital platform enables midwives to access complete patient histories, schedule follow-up visits, and track pregnancy progress.
2. Barangay Health Worker. Through mobile access to the web-based platform, the worker can register new patients, conduct home visits with updated patient information, and efficiently maintain community health records.
Objectives
This research aims to develop and implement a web-based system of prenatal and immunization record that enables real-time input and management of prenatal checkup records and child immunization data for the Barangay Mecolong Health Center. This system will allow healthcare workers to conduct prenatal monitoring and immunization tracking effectively, ensuring that maternal and child health records are preserved digitally for continuous healthcare delivery.
Specific Objectives
The specific objectives of this research are:
1.	To identify the current processes of managing prenatal and immunization records and patient monitoring in the healthcare facility.
2.	To design and develop a web-based system of prenatal and immunization record that enables prenatal monitoring and immunization tracking while maintaining digital patient records.
3.	To evaluate the system's functionality, reliability, and usability in real-world healthcare scenarios by conducting user testing and gathering feedback from healthcare workers and patients.
Scope and Limitation
The proposed system is a web-based system of prenatal and immunization record that enables healthcare workers to manage patient records and conduct prenatal monitoring and immunization tracking at the Barangay Mecolong Health Center. Healthcare workers can create patient profiles and monitor progress throughout the care cycle by utilizing features such as user registration, secure login, and patient tracking. After logging in, the platform displays available services, allowing healthcare workers to access patient records, schedule appointments, and update medical information. The midwife and Barangay Health Workers can approve or update patient care plans accordingly. Patients can receive updates about their appointment schedules and vaccination reminders through the notification system. Additionally, the system features appointment scheduling, immunization tracking, and reporting capabilities. 
The system will focus specifically on prenatal monitoring and immunization tracking for the Barangay Mecolong Health Center, which requires internet connectivity for all operations and depends on SMS gateway services for patient notifications. The platform will be limited to maternal and child health services currently provided by the healthcare facility. The system maintains digital records of prenatal and immunization data that the law allows to be stored electronically. The system will not replace required paper documentation where legal requirements mandate physical record retention; however, it will serve as a supplementary digital platform for improved access and processing efficiency. General medical consultations, emergency care services, and other healthcare programs are closely related medical operations; however, this project will not include them as they serve different healthcare purposes and require separate system architectures. The system will not incorporate telemedicine features such as video consultations or remote patient monitoring, as these components are beyond the scope of this project and would require additional development resources and complexity that may impact the core functionality and timeline of the prenatal and immunization monitoring system.

Definition of Terms
1.	Barangay Health Worker (BHW). A community healthcare worker who registers patients, conducts home visits, and maintains community health records using the system.
2.	Digital Record Management. The process of storing patient healthcare information electronically for easy access and management.
3.	Immunization Card. The physical record showing a child's vaccination history and scheduled immunizations.
4.	Immunization Tracking. Digital tracking of vaccination schedules, records, and compliance for pediatric patients.
5.	Mother's Record Book. The official documentation of prenatal care visits and medical assessments during pregnancy.
6.	Notification System. Automated alerts sent via SMS to inform patients about appointments, vaccinations, and follow-up care.
7.	Patient. Pregnant women and children who receive prenatal care and immunization services tracked by the digital system.
8.	Web-based Prenatal Monitoring System. A digital platform accessible through web browsers that enables healthcare workers to track, manage, and maintain records of pregnancy-related checkups, appointments, and medical assessments.

Web-Based Health Information System
Several web-based health information systems utilize various technological approaches to enhance healthcare management. Building a foundation in this area, Torkudzor, Agbemabiese, and Wellington (2020) detailed a foundational system using PHP, MySQL, CSS, HTML, and JavaScript, deployed over a network using XAMPP and an Apache server. This system was tested across standalone computers, LAN, and WAN configurations, integrating the functionality of locally installed desktop, network, and web applications to ensure patient record availability and timely provision of statistical data. 
Expanding on this approach, developers created the Philippine Immunization Management Information System (IMIS) to address the needs of barangay health centers in the Philippines. Like Torkudzor's system, this system uses PHP for web development and database interaction, MySQL for data management, and front-end technologies such as HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, and JavaScript to provide a user-friendly interface. The system uses an HTTP SMS API to facilitate timely communication with parents, operates on standard hardware to ensure cost-effectiveness in resource-limited environments, and employs Chart.js and DataTables to enhance data visualization and interactivity (Catedrilla, Castillon, Jr., Alonzo, & Vesorio, 2025).
In a move towards more advanced solutions, the developers are developing a Maternal e-Healthcare System that incorporates Laravel (a PHP framework), MongoDB, SQL for database management, LaraAdmin for the UI, and Redis for caching. This system emphasizes scalability, security, and performance optimization through tools like JMeter, marking a significant advancement in the sophistication of these systems. (Dhage, Thakker, & Warhade, 2025).
Similarly, developers build the web-based health information system using Laravel, a PHP framework by Taylor Otwell, for server-side scripting. Key Laravel features include Bundles, Eloquent ORM, Query Builder, Resource Controller, Blade, Migration, Middleware, and Automatic Pagination. Artisan CLI and PHP Composer aided development. The developers utilized a MySQL database management system, employed the Waterfall method for system development, and conducted black-box testing for quality assurance. The system architecture comprises an Apache web server, a PHP application server, and a MySQL database server (Samsinar, Suryati, Sinaga, Lestari, & Afrianny, 2023).
Adding to the range of antenatal care solutions, a web-based antenatal care management platform (ANCMP) using HTML and CSS for the front end, PHP for server-side connections and database management, XAMPP as the server environment, and Bootstrap for responsive design, with Atom code editor used for writing the code (Aliyi et al., 2023). This combination of technologies creates a user-friendly, secure system that effectively manages and stores antenatal care data.

Maternal and Child Health Framework
The development of effective maternal and child health (MCH) interventions and policies relies on several interconnected factors, as highlighted by recent research. (George, Rowe, Barnes, & Kearney, 2021) emphasize the need for transparent reporting in developing mobile health (mHealth) interventions, explicitly citing the Parenting Preemies Support Program. They emphasize the importance of sharing the theoretical basis behind these programs and understanding user perspectives. (Meidani et al., 2022) Echo this user-centered approach by exploring how design thinking helps create integrated maternal and child health information systems (IMCHIS), such as the one they are developing in Iran. They are trying to address issues such as incomplete records and poor data quality, which make managing health information challenging.
These challenges underscore the importance of a continuum of care (CoC) to ensure that women and children receive the right services at the right time and in the right place. (Shibanuma, et al., 2018) Remember that while CoC is an excellent policy framework, how it is usually measured does not always reflect whether women and children adhere to it. To truly understand what affects the health of moms, late-term fetuses, and newborns, Singh et al. (2022) emphasize the importance of a robust MCH framework. Their holistic framework combines areas such as health policy, service delivery, and risk factors to improve outcomes. Finally, they emphasize that they desperately need high-quality MCH data to determine if they are making progress toward Sustainable Development Goal 3. They explain that if our health data is inaccurate, incomplete, late, or inconsistent, we cannot know if we are on the right track. They define key concepts such as data accuracy, completeness, timeliness, and consistency, which are important for monitoring (Lasim, Ansah, & Apaak, 2022).   	
SMS-Based Health Reminders
Parental reminder strategies, particularly those that leverage mobile technology, have emerged as a promising approach to improving immunization outcomes, as highlighted in several recent studies. (Dathini, Sharoni, & Robert, 2022) Conducted a systematic review and meta-analysis, addressing the global challenge of vaccinating children amidst various barriers. Their review of various parental interventions found that mobile technology, particularly SMS reminders, is a cost-effective strategy for improving immunization coverage and timeliness. This strategy also aligns with the findings of Obi-Jeff et al. (2021), who investigated SMS-based reminders (IRISS) in Northern Nigeria. Using the Health Belief Model and the Theory of Planned Behavior, they found that targeted SMS messages could bridge information gaps and improve vaccination uptake, especially when community leaders were involved.
A study took a different approach by creating a web-based vaccination system with SMS reminders for health workers in the Philippines. Their system, which incorporates a GIS map and utilizes the Rapid Application Development Model (RAD), facilitates the management of vaccination data, monitoring of areas, and updates to residents (Osalla, Datolayta, & Soberano, 2023). Gibson et al. (2017) further explored the effectiveness of SMS reminders by combining them with monetary incentives in Kenya. Their study demonstrated that SMS reminders, particularly when accompanied by incentives, significantly enhanced immunization coverage and timeliness, even in areas with already high baseline immunization rates.
Building on this evidence, a study assesses the impact of text message reminders on pediatric immunization rates in urban health centers. Their findings suggest that SMS reminders can be a valuable tool for improving health service utilization, particularly among low-income and diverse populations. However, they acknowledge that the desired immunization rates were not fully achieved (Rosen et al., 2025). These studies highlight the potential of SMS-based health reminders to enhance health service utilization, particularly in increasing vaccination rates, by addressing demand-side barriers such as forgetfulness and lack of awareness.  
Effectiveness of SMS Reminders for Prenatal Care
Researchers and health organizations are increasingly recognizing mobile phone technology as a powerful tool for enhancing maternal and child health, particularly in low- and middle-income countries, and for supporting the UN's Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) to reduce child mortality and improve maternal health by 2030. Mobile phones offer innovative ways to reach even the most remote communities. (Bossman, Johansen, & Zanaboni, 2022) reviewed studies demonstrating that SMS reminders and other mobile interventions increase access to care during pregnancy, childbirth, and after delivery and improve vaccination rates. However, they also emphasize that developing and implementing these mobile health (mhealth) interventions requires careful consideration.
Researchers designed and analyzed a telemonitoring system for high-risk pregnant women. The system alerts healthcare centers through SMS when a high-risk patient is discharged and ensures remote monitoring of her condition. In addition to sending notifications, the system transmits the patient's medical record, post-discharge care recommendations, prescription medications, follow-up schedules, and contact information through a web application.
The study highlights that SMS-based reminders can enhance the management of high-risk pregnancies and improve health outcomes for mothers and infants by facilitating effective remote monitoring and support, as well as addressing barriers to access to high-quality maternal and child healthcare (Nazari, Moayed Rezaie, Yaseri, Sadr, & Nazari, 2024). This focus on SMS reminders is echoed in the work of MacDonald et al. (2022), who conducted a pilot study in Alberta, Canada, to assess the effectiveness and acceptability of SMS text message reminders to improve immunization appointment attendance. 
Systematic reviews and meta-analyses further reinforced the effectiveness of SMS reminders. (Mekonnen, Gelaye, Were, Gashu, & Tilahun, 2019) found a significant positive effect of text message reminders on childhood vaccination coverage, highlighting the potential benefits of incorporating mobile text message reminders into standard childhood immunization management, especially in low- and middle-income countries. (Eze, Lawani, & Acharya, 2021) Specifically focused on low- and middle-income countries (LMICs), the study found that SMS reminders significantly improve childhood immunization coverage and timeliness. SMS reminders are particularly effective in lower-income countries, and sending more than two messages yields better results. These studies demonstrate the potential of SMS reminders as a valuable tool for increasing childhood immunization rates and improving maternal health outcomes.
System Functionality and Reliability Evaluation
The evaluation of health information systems requires comprehensive assessment of multiple quality dimensions, with functionality and reliability serving as foundational attributes that directly impact system effectiveness and user acceptance. Recent research has established robust frameworks and methodologies for evaluating these critical aspects of health information systems.

The HOT-fit (Human, Organization, and Technology-fit) framework provides a comprehensive approach to evaluating health information systems by examining the interplay between technological capabilities, organizational contexts, and human factors. This framework, which has been extensively applied in health information system evaluations since its development, addresses functionality through its technology dimension, reliability through system quality metrics, and usability through human factors assessment. A comprehensive review published in 2020 synthesized evaluation criteria for health information systems using the HOT-fit framework, demonstrating its effectiveness in capturing the multidimensional nature of system quality. The framework evaluates system functionality by assessing whether the technology performs its intended tasks effectively, examines reliability through measures of system stability and data accuracy, and considers how these technical attributes align with organizational workflows and user needs (Yusof et al., 2020).

Building on evaluation frameworks, researchers have developed specific methodologies for assessing hospital information systems. A 2020 study employed the PIECES (Performance, Information, Economy, Control, Efficiency, Service) framework combined with End-User Computing Satisfaction (EUCS) methods to evaluate hospital information system quality. This dual-method approach systematically assessed system functionality by examining whether the system delivers accurate information and performs required tasks efficiently, while reliability was evaluated through performance metrics and system control mechanisms. The study demonstrated that comprehensive evaluation requires examining both technical system attributes and their practical impact on healthcare delivery, with particular attention to how well the system maintains consistent performance under varying operational conditions (Evaluation of Hospital Information System, 2020).

The validation of standardized evaluation instruments has further advanced the field of health information system assessment. Research conducted in Brazil from February to May 2020 validated the National Usability-Focused Health Information System Scale (NuHISS), which measures multiple dimensions of system quality including technical quality, ease of use, and system benefits. This validation study found that technical quality, which encompasses both functionality and reliability, explained 43.5% of users' overall system evaluation. The research emphasized that system reliability—measured through consistent performance, minimal errors, and dependable data processing—forms a critical foundation for user acceptance. When systems fail to perform reliably, even well-designed functionality cannot compensate for user frustration and reduced trust in the system (NuHISS Validation Study, 2020).

Systematic reviews of health information system evaluation methods have highlighted the importance of employing multiple assessment approaches to capture different aspects of system quality. A systematic review covering studies from 2020 to 2022 analyzed usability evaluation methods for hospital information systems and found that comprehensive evaluation requires combining user testing, heuristic evaluation, and standardized assessment tools. While the review focused primarily on usability, it emphasized that functional completeness and system reliability are prerequisites for meaningful usability assessment. Systems that fail to perform their core functions reliably cannot achieve acceptable usability, regardless of interface design quality. The review identified that the most effective evaluations assess functionality through task completion rates, evaluate reliability through error frequency and system uptime metrics, and measure usability through user satisfaction and efficiency indicators (Systematic Review of HIS Evaluation Methods, 2020-2022).

These evaluation frameworks and methodologies provide essential guidance for assessing health information systems comprehensively, ensuring that systems not only possess required functionality but also demonstrate the reliability necessary for critical healthcare applications. The convergence of these research findings emphasizes that effective health information system evaluation must address functionality, reliability, and usability as interconnected quality dimensions rather than isolated attributes.

User Satisfaction and Usability in Health Information Systems
The usability of health information systems (HIS) is a critical factor influencing physician satisfaction, efficiency, and ultimately, patient care. Several recent studies have explored various aspects of HIS usability, employed different methodologies, and focused on diverse healthcare settings. (Hyppönen et al., 2019) Addressed the lack of validated HIS usability questionnaires by developing and validating the National Usability-Focused Health Information System Scale (NuHISS) for physicians. Using cross-sectional data from the Finnish. The study identified seven key factors contributing to HIS usability: technical quality, information quality, feedback, ease of use, benefits, internal, and cross-organizational collaboration. They deemed NuHISS a valuable tool for measuring HIS usability and monitoring its development. Researchers also emphasize the importance of standardized usability assessment, as noted by Hyzy et al. (2022), who conducted a meta-analysis to determine whether the standard System Usability Scale (SUS) distribution is applicable for evaluating Digital Health Apps (DHAs).
The System Usability Scale (SUS), which evaluates user satisfaction and usability, has been applied to the Swedish Patient Accessible Electronic Health Record (PAEHR) system, known as Journalen. Their study, published in JMIR Human Factors, revealed that Journalen has reasonably good usability, with a mean SUS score of 79.81. However, they also noted that further research is needed to explore specific usability areas to ensure usefulness and ease of use (Hägglund & Scandurra, 2021). Kuang-Ming (Kuo, Liu, Talley, & Pan, 2018) adopted a different approach, aiming to identify attributes that enhance physician satisfaction with hospital information systems (HIS). They conducted their study in southern Taiwan and used a model incorporating system, information, and service quality to predict user satisfaction. 
In contrast to the relatively positive findings, a study on the usability of electronic health record (EHR) systems in UK Emergency Departments (EDs) found that none met the internationally validated standard for acceptable usability. Their research, which involved a survey of members and fellows from the Royal College of Emergency Medicine, assessed the usability of EHR systems using the System Usability Scale (SUS). The findings indicated that usability was associated with the specific EHR system and the healthcare organization implementing it, highlighting the importance of user satisfaction and usability in health information systems (Bloom, Pott, Thomas, Gaunt, & Hughes, 2021). These studies emphasized the importance of usability in health information systems and the need for ongoing evaluation and improvement to enhance user experience and optimize healthcare delivery.

Cloud Backup for Medical Data Storage

In modern healthcare, where patient information is digital and remote care is common, cloud computing has become the central nervous system, not just a simple storage closet. Cao et al. (2021) describe cloud backup as the essential, behind-the-scenes engine that powers health monitoring systems using everyday internet-connected devices. Their work shows how vital signs like blood pressure and heart rate, collected from sensors, travel securely to a central cloud platform. This system does more than just store numbers; it builds a dynamic health record that helps doctors identify long-term trends, conduct remote consultations, and provide additional services, all while proving to be highly reliable.

The researchers then build on this model by focusing on its security gaps. While the first study highlights the benefits of sending data to the cloud, the second addresses the risks, such as information being intercepted during transfer or misused within the cloud itself. To create a stronger system, Chang et al. propose a smarter backup and recovery plan. This strategy uses a secure Internet of Things (IoT) framework and a powerful data-processing tool called Spark. Their method employs clever techniques to eliminate duplicate data and manage information more efficiently, ensuring that critical health data remains secure and can be recovered quickly after any problem Chang et al. (2021).
The experimental results from Chang et al. (2021) provide strong, real-world proof for the stability that Cao et al. (2021) described. In their tests, the enhanced backup system successfully kept data loss during transmission below 5%. This low loss rate is a key indicator of a robust system and directly supports the smooth, uninterrupted performance required for patient monitoring. It confirms that a well-designed cloud backup can serve as a trustworthy foundation for critical applications like telehealth.

In conclusion, these two studies together present a complete picture of cloud backup in healthcare. Cao et al. (2021) demonstrate why it is indispensable for modern medicine, and Chang et al. (2021) explain how to implement it securely and effectively. Their combined research confirms that as healthcare goes digital, cloud backup is not a passive storage bin but an active guardian of life-saving information.

3.4.2	Non-Functional Requirements

3.4.2.1 Usability
The system must provide an intuitive, user-friendly interface that enables healthcare workers to operate efficiently regardless of their technical proficiency. It should minimize errors and reduce the need for extensive training, allowing midwives and Barangay Health Workers to navigate and use the system effectively with minimal guidance.

3.4.2.2 Reliability
The platform must deliver consistent and accurate results in managing patient records, generating reports, and tracking prenatal and immunization schedules. It should ensure continuous system availability with a minimum uptime of 85%, dependable performance, and robust data integrity through regular backups and recovery mechanisms.

3.4.2.3 Performance
The system must process user inputs, data retrieval, and reporting operations efficiently, ensuring minimal downtime and responsive interaction across multiple users simultaneously. Fast access to patient records and real-time updates are essential to support timely healthcare decisions and prevent service delays.

3.4.2.4 Accessibility
As a web-based solution, the platform must be accessible from different devices and locations with stable internet connectivity, ensuring continuous operation for healthcare workers at the Barangay Mecolong Health Center and during home visits.

3.4.2.5 Security
The system must protect sensitive patient information through role-based access control, secure authentication mechanisms, and encrypted data transmission. It should comply with healthcare data privacy regulations and prevent unauthorized access to prenatal and immunization records.
